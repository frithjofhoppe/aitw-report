\subsection{Research Design}

The report follows a qualitative, document-based comparative approach. Official provider documentation on landing zones or functionally equivalent foundation systems was analyzed, together with open technical sources on cloud reference architectures, multi-cloud environments, and Infrastructure as Code. The aim was not to measure implementation quality, but to reconstruct the underlying conceptual and architectural models.

\subsection{Provider Selection}

The concepts of Microsoft Azure, Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, Alibaba Cloud, and IBM Cloud were examined. The selection is justified by their market relevance, their enterprise-oriented documentation, and the fact that all five providers offer explicit or functionally equivalent foundation concepts for secure and controlled cloud adoption.

\subsection{Comparison Framework}

The comparison framework covers security, governance, scalability, resource organization, and standardization and automation. These dimensions were selected because they appear repeatedly in almost all provider sources and because they cover the core requirements of modern cloud foundation architectures. The analysis examined which organizational and technical guardrails each provider defines and how strongly these guardrails are connected to later workload operation.

\subsection{Analysis Procedure}

For each provider, definitions, purpose, and structural elements were first extracted. Statements on hierarchy, identity, policy inheritance, logging, compliance, automation, and workload deployment were then assigned to the five comparison dimensions. Only after this mapping was the synthesis developed. This procedure helped avoid implicitly treating a single provider as the norm.

\subsection{Methodological Limitations}

A first limitation lies in the nature of official documentation: it is normative, product-oriented, and uneven in its level of detail. A second limitation is that this version does not include practical validation through case studies or implementations. The results should therefore be understood as a conceptual comparison rather than an empirical assessment of real-world landing zone operation. For the final version, the sources used in this report should be linked to the corresponding BibTeX entries in \texttt{zotero.bib}.
